My Design Process

01

Understand the Expectations

The first step I like to take is to make sure that there's a shared understanding of expectations, requirements, deliverables, and deadlines between myself and the team or stakeholders.

02

Define the Problem(s)

Collecting multiple perspectives often sparks conversations and problem statements which provides direction on who the problem affects, how it does, and why itâ€™s important to solve the problem.

03

Research and Competitor Analysis

When available, I look at any data or reports and gather other useful information that would uncover user behaviors, needs, and motivations, in addition to researching the competitors.

04

Develop Solution(s)

Ideation can be done alone but I value team collaboration as it can generate more diverse ideas. I like to take notes, sketch, and watch ideas bounce off one another while focusing on quantity over quality.

05

Wireframe or Prototype Creation

During this stage, I'm able to communicate and test my ideas before deep diving into the visual design. Depending on the scope, the degree of detail and fidelity may vary.

06

Implementing the Visual Design

My favorite part! This is where everything comes together. Not only does an interface need to be usable, the way it looks also affects how users experience them.

How I Work

Power in Minimalism

I strive to help users focus on their goals without getting distracted or overwhelmed and ultimately abandoning the task at hand. When designing I try to contain components that are relevant and provide a clear and visible path to navigate to and through other content. With more complex tasks, I work to break it down into smaller steps in order to decrease a users' cognitive load.

A Shared Understanding

With my background as a Web Designer and experience in front-end development, I have an understanding of how a developer thinks. Having this understanding helps me be more flexible when working within a multidisciplinary team as I can sympathize with the developers and can accurately communicate to them my visual ideas and needs more easily.
